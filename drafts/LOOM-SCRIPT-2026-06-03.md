# Day14 OS — 4-Minute Demo Loom Script
# 2026-06-03

Total runtime target: 4:00. Shot in one take if possible; second take if the cold open lands flat. Talking head bottom-right corner, screen recording primary. Read the cold open from the prose below verbatim — the rest is beat-by-beat against the actual product.

---

## 0:00 — Cold open (15 seconds, talking head full-frame)

> I run six businesses. I'm one person. The way I do that is a system I built called Day14 OS, and the short version is: every business I run is a tenant in one stack, every cross-tenant fact lives in one query, and an inbox tells me what needs a human decision instead of me going looking for it.

> What I'm about to show you is the actual admin I use every morning. Not a mock. Not a roadmap. The thing that's currently routing six businesses' worth of work through one operator's attention. Four minutes. Watch what a team of one looks like when the seams are gone.

*(Beat. Cut to screen.)*

---

## 0:15 — Open the admin inbox

- Show the URL bar typing `day14.us/admin` (or local: `localhost:3000/admin`).
- Land on the inbox. Voiceover: "This is the first screen I open every day. It's the only screen I have to open every day."
- Hover the kind filters at the top — Approval · Decision · Review · FYI. "Four kinds. Anything that isn't one of these doesn't reach me."

## 0:35 — Approve three items live

- Click into the first item: a brand-hero image generated by a scheduled agent for the realty tenant. Read the title aloud. Hit `A` to approve. Show the toast.
- Second item: a draft tweet from the content tenant. Read the first line. Hit `A`.
- Third item: a Stripe refund draft from AlignMD. Voiceover: "This one is money. The agent drafted it; I have to tap to send. Every money-moving action requires one human tap. Agents draft, humans publish."
- Hit `A`. Three approvals in roughly 25 seconds.

## 1:00 — Switch the tenant filter

- Top-right tenant dropdown. Cycle from "All" through "alignmd," "splash-jacks," "realty," "content." Voiceover: "Same layout, same shortcuts, same inbox — different tenant, different data. Multi-tenant from the metal up."
- Land back on "All."

## 1:25 — Fire a deploy

- Click into the deploy strip. Show the per-tenant deploy status (last commit SHA, time-since, build status).
- Click "Deploy" on the day14-os tenant. Voiceover: "Deploy strip. Tells me at a glance which tenant pushed last and when. One click pushes the next one."
- Show the build firing. Cut to the live Vercel/build log briefly. "Build's running. I don't need to watch it; the inbox will tell me when it finishes or fails."

## 1:55 — Scroll the work-log

- Navigate to `/work-log` or the equivalent admin route.
- Scroll the last 14 days. Voiceover: "This is the auto-generated work-log. Every shipped change writes a line. Every scheduled task writes a line. Every approved inbox item writes a line. The work is the receipts."
- Stop on a day with 6–8 entries. Read two of them. "That's a real day. Nothing in here is hand-written by me."

## 2:25 — Scheduled-task panel

- Open the scheduled tasks panel. Show maybe 12–15 named tasks across tenants.
- Voiceover: "Agents run overnight. They draft. They parse. They reconcile. They write evidence to disk and the next morning's briefing reads the disk to check. Return-status is a lie. Disk is the truth."
- Hover one task to show its last-run timestamp and exit status.

## 2:50 — One tenant deep-dive (AlignMD)

- Switch tenant filter to "alignmd." Navigate to the AlignMD-specific admin surface.
- Show the credential intake queue. Click one nurse's packet. Show the parsed license #, state, expiration, board cert. Voiceover: "This is a real product running on the same substrate. Different domain, different UI, same OS underneath. The substrate doesn't care what the tenant sells."

## 3:15 — Skills registry / agent dispatch

- Open the skills panel. Show the named plays — 50+ skills, each with a description.
- Voiceover: "The OS knows 59 named plays. When an agent needs to do something, it dispatches a skill instead of guessing. Each one is testable. Each one is named. Each one is honest about what it does and doesn't do."

## 3:35 — Pull back to the landing page

- Navigate to `day14.us`. Show the hero.
- Voiceover: "That's the system. This is the landing. If you're one operator who wants to ship like twenty, the waitlist is here."

---

## 3:45 — CTA close (15 seconds, talking head full-frame)

> The waitlist is at day14.us. If 50 operators want this by Sunday at 2 PM Eastern, I start onboarding the first cohort next week — I'll write a setup guide, hand-walk you through tenant config, and figure out what the productized version of this looks like with you in the loop. Fewer, and the OS stays private tooling that keeps doing its job. Both outcomes are useful. Either way, the substrate keeps running.

> One person. Twenty-person output. The link's in the description and the pinned tweet. See you on the other side.

*(End card: day14.us logo + waitlist URL, 3 seconds hold, cut.)*
