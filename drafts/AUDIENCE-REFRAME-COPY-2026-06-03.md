# Audience reframe — build-studio positioning
# Updated 2026-06-03 evening after the pivot from SaaS-product → build-studio-with-OS.
# This doc captures the strategic shift + headline candidates already shipped to the live site.

---

## The shift

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| Day14 OS was a SaaS subscription product ($79/$299/$999/mo per-tenant) | Day14 is a **build studio** that ships sites and apps on its own OS |
| Target audience: "solopreneurs running multiple businesses" (TAM ~100) | Target audience: **local businesses + founders + operators** (TAM ~10K+) |
| Primary CTA: "Join the waitlist" | Primary CTA: **"Book a 20-min scope call"** |
| Pricing: 3 SaaS tiers | Pricing: **4 build tiers ($1.5k → custom)** + bundled ops |
| Sunday signal metric: 50+ waitlist signups | Sunday signal metric (new): **3-5 real scope-call requests** |

## What customers Day14 now serves

**Spark tier ($1,500 · 5 days):**
- Local tutors, fitness coaches, lawyers, dentists
- Small contractors (HVAC, landscaping, plumbing, cleaning)
- Solo professionals (consultants, therapists, real-estate agents)
- Anyone who needs a single beautiful page with lead capture but $9k is out of reach

**Studio tier ($9,000 · 14 days):**
- Founders launching SaaS or D2C
- Small businesses upgrading from Wix/Squarespace
- Newsletter writers / content brands needing real editorial design
- Anyone who needs a real multi-page marketing site with blog + lead capture

**Platform tier ($24,000 · 4 weeks):**
- Operators launching real software businesses
- Multi-tenant or single-tenant SaaS needing portal + admin + billing
- Service businesses that need a customer portal beside their marketing site
- Anyone whose product needs to be operated, not just shown

**Custom tier (scoped):**
- Multi-tenant marketplaces
- Industry-specific platforms
- Anything that requires deep Day14 OS access (agents, verifier, full work-log)

- "One operator. Six businesses. One operating system." — autobiographical, doesn't pitch a service

## What this changes about the Sunday signal experiment

The original test: "50+ waitlist signups by Sunday 14:00 EDT = start onboarding."

Under the new positioning, the right signal is different:

- **3-5 real scope-call requests by Sunday** = strong signal, start booking July
- **1-2 scope-call requests** = soft signal, refocus distribution before scaling acquisition
- **0 scope-call requests** = positioning/distribution is broken, pivot before pushing harder

A scope-call request is worth ~10x a waitlist signup at this price-point, so the numerator can be lower.
