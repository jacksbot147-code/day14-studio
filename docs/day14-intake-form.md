# Day14 — Customer Intake Form

> Sent the moment the deposit clears. Drops into Typeform or Notion verbatim.
> The Customer fills this out *once*. After this, no more "send me your logo
> again" emails — every input we need to start the build lives here.
>
> Time to complete: 25–35 minutes. Designed to be the only thing the Customer
> has to do until day 13.

---

## Part 1 — About your business

**1. Business name (legal + DBA, if different)**
> Example: "Splash Jacks Pools LLC" (legal), "Splash Jacks" (DBA)

**2. One-sentence description of what you do.**
> Plain English. No marketing fluff. We'll write the polished version.

**3. Who is your customer?**
> Be specific. "Pool owners in Naples with single-family homes" beats "homeowners."

**4. What are the services or products you offer? (List up to 10)**
> Include pricing for each — even if it's a range. We'll style this into a pricing page.

**5. Where do you operate?**
> City/cities, zip codes, or radius from a central address.
> For SITE: this drives the SEO landing pages.

**6. Years in business + team size**
> Optional but helps with copy. "Solo since 2024" / "Family-owned since 1998 / 6 employees."

---

## Part 2 — Brand assets

**7. Logo**
> Attach the highest-resolution version you have (SVG > PNG > JPG).
> No logo? Tell us "skip logo" and we'll use a styled wordmark.

**8. Brand colors**
> Hex codes if you know them. Otherwise paste 1–3 photos that capture the vibe.

**9. Brand voice — pick the closest descriptor**
> ☐ Plain, confident, no-nonsense
> ☐ Warm, friendly, community-focused
> ☐ Premium, polished, sophisticated
> ☐ Playful, colorful, energetic
> ☐ Technical, precise, expert
> ☐ Other: _______

**10. Five photos**
> Real photos of your work, your team, your space — whatever is true.
> Phone photos are fine. We process them. Avoid stock photography.

**11. Any existing brand materials**
> Existing website URL, social media handles, business card scan, brochure — anything we should reference for visual continuity.

---

## Part 3 — How customers find and pay you today

**12. How do customers currently book / contact you?**
> ☐ Phone calls    ☐ Website form    ☐ Email
> ☐ Walk-ins       ☐ Referrals       ☐ Other: ____

**13. How do you currently get paid?**
> ☐ Stripe          ☐ Square          ☐ Cash + Venmo
> ☐ Other processor: ____             ☐ Not online yet

**14. Do you have an existing Stripe account?**
> ☐ Yes — share the dashboard email so we can wire it
> ☐ No — we'll set one up under your business name during the build

**15. What tools are you using today that we'll be replacing?**
> Jobber, Housecall Pro, GoHighLevel, Squarespace, Mailchimp, etc.
> This helps us plan the data migration if one is needed.

---

## Part 4 — The build

**16. Domain**
> ☐ I own {{domain}} and can transfer DNS control
> ☐ I want to buy {{my-preferred-domain}} — please register in my name
> ☐ I have no preference, recommend one

**17. Email address for customer communications**
> e.g. "hello@yourbusiness.com" — we'll set this up if you don't have it.

**18. Phone number for SMS notifications (Twilio)**
> The business phone customers will see SMS reminders coming from.
> If you don't have a dedicated business number, we'll provision one.

**19. AI chatbot — what should it know how to answer?**
> List the top 10 questions customers ask you today. The chatbot is trained
> on your services, pricing, and FAQs. The better this list, the smarter it sounds.

**20. Anything explicitly off-limits for the chatbot?**
> e.g. "Don't quote prices below $X" / "Never schedule emergency calls — always escalate."

---

## Part 5 — Platform-tier only (skip if Site or Portal)

**21. Do your customers receive recurring service?**
> Frequency? (weekly / biweekly / monthly / on-demand)
> Number of customers in current rotation?

**22. What does a typical job look like?**
> Walk us through one. Inputs, outputs, photos, measurements, anything.

**23. Who else needs admin access besides you?**
> Names + roles. We'll set up role-based logins.

**24. Existing operational tools we should integrate or migrate?**
> Spreadsheets, QuickBooks, Calendars, etc.

---

## Part 6 — Customizations + open questions

**25. What does your ideal day-14 launch look like?**
> Open answer. Describe the moment you'd consider this a success.

**26. What worries you about this project?**
> Be honest. Things you've been burned on before. Things you don't want repeated. Things you've never been able to find a vendor to handle.

**27. Anything else we should know?**
> NDAs, legal restrictions, family-business politics, competitors to avoid, off-limit colors. Speak freely — this stays between us.
